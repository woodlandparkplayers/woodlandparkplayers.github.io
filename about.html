About

Woodland Park Players was formed by a small group of parents looking for a way to find greater community and raise funds for art programs in the Seattle Public Schools. The Woodland Park Players has grown in a short time, into an active community theater company that committed itself to family-oriented musical productions as well as to support our local schools.
According to our by-laws, the Players is a group of adult members (parents, teachers, other residents) who live in the Woodland Park neighborhood and who join together to produce a musical show each year. In addition, the Players must donate its proceeds to school or school district needs. Woodland Park Players dues are annually $40/person, and new members are always welcome. 

To be a member of the Woodland Park Players, several requirements and duties must be met:

1) Ensemble Eligibility Criteria
·      Member of WPP’s inaugural cast (2016 Oklahoma!) –OR--
·      Current or former staff, parent/guardian or former student (over 16) of West Woodland Elementary School –OR--
·      Live within the WPP defined geozone, which is the area:
o   south of N. 80th St.
o   west of Aurora Ave N/SR-99
o   north of N. 50th St.
o   east of 8th Ave NW

	a. Ensemble (min. commitment, at least 2 scenes): 
	·      To be eligible to audition for the ensemble, you must fall into one of the three required criteria stated above, (meaning no one can be in the ensemble who does not fit Ensemble Eligibility Criteria);
	·      In the event that we have more auditioners than we have parts, final casting decision will be at director’s discretion. (meaning you can be cut);
	·      In the event you are NOT cast in this year’s production, please know there is a place for you in the Woodland Park Players! We need lots of help behind the scenes---sets, costumes, props, lighting, craft services, ad sales, front of the house, etc.  And if you join the crew and audition again next year, you will be considered with higher priority. 
	·      You must commit to the rehearsal schedule and sign a “commitment statement” at our WPP Mandatory Business Meeting, December 5, 2016.

	b. Supporting Role (4+scenes, larger speaking roles and more rehearsal commitment)
	·      Director will be strongly encouraged to cast using the Ensemble Eligibility Criteria, but ultimately it’s director’s discretion.
	·      You must commit to the rehearsal schedule and sign a “commitment statement” at our WPP Business Meeting.

	c. Lead Role (5+ scenes, major dialogue and max # of rehearsals)
	·      Director will be strongly encouraged to cast using the Ensemble Eligibility Criteria, but ultimately it’s director’s discretion.
	·      You must commit to the rehearsal schedule and sign a “commitmentstatement” at our WPP Business Meeting.

2) Annual Dues
• Pay annual family dues of $40 for the first adult member, plus $10 for each additional adult;

3) Tickets
• Commit to selling at least 10 tickets (total) for the three performances March 17 & 18.
